    Consultations (including draft legislation and guidance)

    development

    This is currently development because more research is needed.

    Document type

    Consultations

    When to use

    Consultations are any content we ask customers to comment/respond to within a set period of time.

    Examples of types of consultations:

    • formal policy consultations
    • technical consultations, for example draft legislation, draft regulations or draft guidance

    GDS guidance on using the consultations format in Whitehall.

    Formal consultations
    Field What to enter Example
    Title The title of the policy Simplifying the administration of Alcohol Duty
    Summary Read the document and summarise what it is about. A consultation on proposed changes to the administration procedures for those who produce, process or store alcohol subject to Excise Duty.
    Body text An 80-100 word summary should be provided by the policy team, which can be edited to meet the GDS style guide. Always try to include:
    • a summary of the key overall questions being asked
    • "This consultation will be of interest to…”
    		 The consultation is seeking opinion on the following areas:
    • changes to registration requirements
    • alignment of return and payment dates
    • the current position for excise duty deferment including guarantees
    The government is keen to obtain views from a wide range of stakeholders to establish clear evidence-based rationale for its decisions.
    Opening and closing date The SME should provide a publication date.

    Set the time for 9:30am as default (unless SME asks for another start time)

    The SME should provide a closing date.

    The closing time should be set as 23:45, not 00:00 (which will close the consultation a day early)

    		 9:30am on 16 February 2017 to 11:45pm on 26 April 2017
    Response methods Formal consultations will usually have both an email and postal address.
    Documents to publish Publish as HTML or ODT.

    Note: They can’t be changed.
    Technical consultations (draft legislation, regulations or guidance)
    Field What to enter Example
    Title Draft legislation: [description of content] Draft legislation: the Fulfilment Businesses (Approval Scheme) Regulations 2018
    Summary Summarise the main points of the consultation. Include technical consultation to help customers identify this type of content. This technical consultation seeks comments on the draft statutory instrument for the Fulfilment House Due Diligence Scheme.
    Body text An 80-100 word summary should be provided by the policy team, which can be edited to meet the GDS style guide. Always try to include:
    • a summary of the key overall questions being asked
    • "This consultation will be of interest to…”
    		 This technical consultation seeks comments from stakeholders on the draft statutory instrument. In particular, we seek a better understanding of:
    1. The impact on fulfilment businesses in applying the obligations of the scheme, including record-keeping requirements
    2. Whether the provisions for group registrations in Part 5 will be beneficial to group companies
    3. The impact of the scheme on small and micro businesses This is of interest to businesses in the warehousing, storage, fulfilment, imported goods and transport sectors, as well as those involved in e-commerce.
    Opening and closing date The SME should provide a publication date. Set the time for 9:30am as default (unless SME asks for another start time)

    The SME should provide a closing date.

    The closing time should be set as 23:45, not 00:00 (which will close the consultation a day early)

    		 9:30am on 16 February 2017 to 11:45pm on 26 April 2017
    Response methods Technical consultations will usually only have an email address to enter.
    Documents to publish Publish as an HTML or odt.

    Add ‘DRAFT’ watermark to the document.

    Note: They can’t be changed.

    Summary of responses

    There are 2 stages to a consultation response:

    • public feedback
    • final outcome

    Usually HMRC only publishes the final outcome, with the document summarising both what the feedback was, and what the next steps will be.

    Summary of responses should be published on the original consultation page.
